# Customer Centricity Checklist
Clone: @peter-fader (Tier 0)

Validate customer-centric strategy before implementation.

## 1. Customer Identification

### 1.1 Best Customer Definition
- [ ] Defined "best customer" using CLV, not just revenue
- [ ] Identified top 20% customers driving 80% of value
- [ ] RFM segmentation completed and validated
- [ ] Segment sizes are actionable (not too small)

### 1.2 Customer Heterogeneity
- [ ] Recognized that customers are NOT equal
- [ ] Different treatment for different value tiers
- [ ] Resource allocation matches customer value
- [ ] Avoided "one-size-fits-all" approaches

## 2. CLV Foundation

### 2.1 CLV Calculation
- [ ] Historical CLV calculated per customer
- [ ] Projected CLV model validated
- [ ] Confidence intervals documented
- [ ] Model updated at least quarterly

### 2.2 CLV Integration
- [ ] CLV used in acquisition decisions
- [ ] CLV used in retention prioritization
- [ ] CLV used in support SLA definition
- [ ] Marketing attribution weighted by CLV

## 3. Four Pillars Assessment

### 3.1 Customer Acquisition
- [ ] Acquiring customers that LOOK LIKE best customers
- [ ] CAC compared to projected CLV
- [ ] Channel attribution includes CLV weighting
- [ ] Not optimizing for volume at expense of quality

### 3.2 Customer Retention
- [ ] Retention efforts prioritized by customer value
- [ ] Churn prediction model exists
- [ ] Proactive outreach for at-risk high-value customers
- [ ] Win-back campaigns targeted, not mass

### 3.3 Customer Development
- [ ] Upsell/cross-sell based on propensity AND value
- [ ] Not annoying low-propensity customers
- [ ] Expansion revenue tracked per segment
- [ ] Product usage correlated to development opportunity

### 3.4 Customer Equity
- [ ] Total customer equity calculated
- [ ] Customer equity growth tracked over time
- [ ] Used in business valuation discussions
- [ ] Board/investors understand the metric

## 4. Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### 4.1 Product Centricity Traps
- [ ] NOT treating all customers equally
- [ ] NOT obsessing over NPS without CLV context
- [ ] NOT chasing market share at expense of margin
- [ ] NOT optimizing for engagement without value

### 4.2 Common Mistakes
- [ ] NOT using average revenue per user (ARPU) alone
- [ ] NOT ignoring customer heterogeneity
- [ ] NOT over-investing in low-value segments
- [ ] NOT under-investing in high-value segments

## 5. Organizational Alignment

### 5.1 Metrics Alignment
- [ ] Teams measured on customer value metrics
- [ ] Incentives aligned with CLV growth
- [ ] Cross-functional view of customer
- [ ] Single source of truth for customer data

### 5.2 Cultural Readiness
- [ ] Leadership understands customer centricity
- [ ] Finance speaks CLV language
- [ ] Marketing targets by value, not volume
- [ ] Product considers value in roadmap
