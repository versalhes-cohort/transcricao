## Part 1: Purpose

Build a growing audience of warm leads through free content that hooks, retains, and rewards attention. The Content Creation process creates an **accumulating asset** - the audience itself - that compounds over time and makes all other advertising more effective.

**Core Equation:**
```
Audience Growth = Content Units x Volume x Consistency

Example:
- 1 piece/day x 30 days = 30 content units
- Each unit hooks, retains, rewards audience
- Audience grows → More leads → More money
```

**Business Impact:** "Construir uma audiencia e a coisa mais valiosa que ja fiz." (Building an audience is the most valuable thing I've ever done.) - Alex Hormozi, p.67

**Key Insight:** "O conteudo que voce cria nao e o ativo que se acumula - a audiencia e. Entao, mesmo que o conteudo possa desaparecer com o tempo, sua audiencia continua crescendo." (The content you create isn't the asset that accumulates - the audience is. So even though content may disappear over time, your audience keeps growing.) - p.65

## Part 2: Scope

### When This SOP Applies
- Building audience through free content (social media, podcasts, videos, blogs)
- Creating content units that hook, retain, and reward attention
- Scaling content production across platforms
- Monetizing audience through integrated or intermittent offers

### When This SOP Does NOT Apply
- Warm outreach (direct personal contact) - covered in other playbook
- Cold outreach (direct advertising) - covered in other playbook
- Paid ads (covered separately)
- Sales calls/closing (covered in Lead Nurture SOP)

### Business Types
- Any business wanting to build an audience
- Creators, coaches, consultants, agencies
- B2B and B2C
- Product and service businesses

## Part 5: Process Steps

### PHASE I: HOOK - GETTING ATTENTION

#### Step 1.1: Choose Your Topic
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Select topic from five categories based on your experience |
| **Cognitive Type** | Synthesis (connecting experience to audience needs) |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Requires personal experience and judgment |
| **Executor** | Human |
| **Precondition** | Personal experiences documented |
| **Output** | Topic selected for content creation |
| **Guardrails** | "So existe um de voce. A maneira mais facil de se diferenciar e dizer algo que ninguem mais pode dizer." (There's only one of you. The easiest way to differentiate is to say something no one else can say.) - p.121 |
| **Decision Rule** | Use personal experiences; speak from what YOU did, not what others should do |

**Five Topic Categories:**

**a) Passado Distante (Distant Past):**
- Important life lessons from your past
- Connect wisdom to your product/service
- "De a eles a historia sem a cicatriz." (Give them the story without the scar.) - p.123

**b) Passado Recente (Recent Past):**
- Talk about what you just did or what happened
- Review your calendar from last week
- Meetings, social interactions, conversations with clients
- Testimonials and case studies fit here - p.134-136

**c) Presente (Present):**
- Ideas as they come to you in the moment
- "Sempre tenha uma maneira de gravar suas ideias ao alcance dos bracos." (Always have a way to record your ideas within arm's reach.) - p.137
- "Eu anoto minhas ideias publicamente." (I note my ideas publicly.) - p.139

**d) Tendencias (Trends):**
- What's trending now
- Apply your experience to current events
- "Falar sobre coisas da moda e muito eficaz para ganhar a atencao de uma audiencia mais ampla." (Talking about trending things is very effective for gaining attention from a wider audience.) - p.141

**e) Fabricado (Fabricated):**
- Create experiences specifically for content
- Example: "Vivi com $100 por um mes. Eis como." (I lived on $100 for a month. Here's how.) - p.145
- Costs more time but can have biggest returns - p.143

#### Step 1.2: Craft Your Headline
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Create headline using news-proven components to capture attention |
| **Cognitive Type** | Creativity + Analysis |
| **Automation Potential** | MEDIUM - Templates help, but creativity needed |
| **Executor** | Hybrid |
| **Precondition** | Topic selected |
| **Output** | Headline that gives reason to consume |
| **Guardrails** | Include at least two components from the seven proven elements |
| **Decision Rule** | "Eles a usam para pesar a probabilidade de obterem uma recompensa por consumir seu conteudo versus outro." (They use it to weigh the probability of getting a reward from consuming your content versus another.) - p.149 |

**Seven Headline Components (from meta-analysis of news):**

| Component | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Recencia** (Recency) | Most recent possible | "Aconteceu ha uma hora" vs "ha um ano" - p.155 |
| **Relevancia** (Relevance) | Personally significant to audience | "Enfermeiras prestam mais atencao a coisas que afetam enfermeiras" - p.157 |
| **Celebridade** (Celebrity) | Including prominent people | "Normalmente, nao nos importariamos com o que outro humano come... Mas se for Jeff Bezos, nos importamos." - p.159 |
| **Proximidade** (Proximity) | Close to home geographically | "Uma casa pegando fogo do outro lado do pais nao chama sua atencao. Se for seu vizinho, certamente chama." - p.161 |
| **Conflito** (Conflict) | Opposing ideas, people, nature | "Abacaxi vs sem abacaxi na pizza? Conflito!" - p.163 |
| **Incomum** (Unusual) | Strange, unique, rare, bizarre | "Se esta fora da norma, as pessoas prestam mais atencao." - p.165 |
| **Em andamento** (Ongoing) | Stories still in progress | "Se alguem entra em trabalho de parto, as pessoas querem atualizacoes a cada dez minutos" - p.167 |

#### Step 1.3: Match Your Format
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Format content to match platform expectations and audience preferences |
| **Cognitive Type** | Perception (reading platform norms) |
| **Automation Potential** | HIGH - Templates and repurposing tools |
| **Executor** | Worker (formatting) + Human (creative decisions) |
| **Precondition** | Topic and headline defined |
| **Output** | Content formatted for target platform |
| **Guardrails** | "Voce tem que fazer seu conteudo parecer com o que eles esperam que os recompense." (You have to make your content look like what they expect will reward them.) - p.179 |
| **Decision Rule** | Study the best content on the platform targeting your market; match that format |

**Format Matching Process:**
1. Identify target platform
2. Study top-performing content in your niche
3. Note visual style, length, structure
4. Adapt your content to match expectations
5. "Formate seu conteudo para a plataforma primeiro. Entao, ajuste-o para fisgar sua audiencia ideal." - p.181

#### Step 2.1: Use Lists
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|

| **Cognitive Type** | Organization + Synthesis |

| **Output** | List-structured content |
| **Guardrails** | "Dar o numero de itens listados em sua manchete, ou nos primeiros segundos do seu conteudo, diz as pessoas o que esperar." - p.191 |

| **Guardrails** | "Passos sao acoes que devem ser feitas em uma ordem especifica para obter um resultado." - p.199 |

#### Step 2.3: Use Stories
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|

| **Output** | Story-structured content |
| **Guardrails** | "Historias que valem a pena contar geralmente tem alguma licao ou conclusao para o ouvinte." - p.201 |
| **Decision Rule** | Stories generate curiosity because people want to know what happens next |

|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Combine lists, steps, and stories for complex content |
| **Cognitive Type** | Synthesis + Creativity |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Requires creative judgment |
| **Executor** | Human |
| **Precondition** | Multiple retention structures identified |
| **Output** | Complex, engaging content |
| **Guardrails** | "Voce pode usar listas, passos e historias sozinhos ou entrelaca-los." - p.205 |
| **Decision Rule** | Your creativity is the only limit; great content weaves all three together |

**Interweaving Examples:**
- Lists within steps with a story for each item
- Stories to reinforce the value of a step
- A list of stories

### PHASE IV: MONETIZATION

#### Step 4.1: Master the Give:Ask Ratio
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Maintain high give ratio to build goodwill before asking |
| **Cognitive Type** | Strategic judgment |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Requires strategic decision |
| **Executor** | Human |
| **Precondition** | Audience established |
| **Output** | Sustainable monetization strategy |
| **Guardrails** | Television: 47min give, 13min ask (3.5:1). Growing platforms give much more. |
| **Decision Rule** | "O momento em que voce comeca a pedir dinheiro e o momento em que decide desacelerar seu crescimento." - p.271 |

**Ratios:**
- Television: ~3.5:1 (give:ask) - mature, monetizing platform
- Facebook: ~4:1 (content:ads) - mature platform
- Growing platforms: "dão dão dão... dão dão dão... talvez peçam" - p.259

**Best Strategy:** "De ate que eles pecam." (Give until they ask.) - p.263

#### Step 4.2: Choose Offer Integration Method
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Decide between integrated offers or intermittent offers |
| **Cognitive Type** | Strategic judgment |
| **Automation Potential** | MEDIUM - Can template the approach |
| **Executor** | Hybrid |
| **Precondition** | Offer ready (lead magnet or main offer) |
| **Output** | Offer integration plan |
| **Guardrails** | Monitor if audience growth slows after introducing asks |
| **Decision Rule** | Long-form = integrated; Short-form = intermittent - p.295 |

**Integrated Offers:**
- Announce in every piece of content
- Keep give:ask ratio high within each piece
- Example: 3 x 30-second ads in 1-hour podcast = 58.5min give : 1.5min ask - p.285
- Place CTAs after valuable moments or at end - p.289

**Intermittent Offers:**
- Many pure "give" pieces
- Occasional "ask" piece
- Example: 10 give posts, 1 ask post - p.293

### PHASE V: SCALING

#### Step 5.1: Choose Scaling Strategy
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Select depth-first or breadth-first scaling approach |
| **Cognitive Type** | Strategic judgment |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Business decision |
| **Executor** | Human |
| **Precondition** | One platform working |
| **Output** | Scaling roadmap |
| **Guardrails** | Both approaches are valid; choose based on resources and goals |
| **Decision Rule** | Existing business with resources = breadth-first; Starting out = depth-first |

**Depth-First (Profundidade depois largura):**
1. Post on one relevant platform
2. Post regularly on that platform
3. Maximize quality AND quantity on that platform
4. Add another platform while maintaining the first
5. Repeat until all platforms maximized - p.319

*Advantages:* Leverage accumulation, less resources needed
*Disadvantages:* Single channel risk, no omnipresence effect

**Breadth-First (Largura depois profundidade):**
1. Post on one relevant platform
2. Post regularly
3. Move to next platform (maintaining previous)
4. Continue until on all relevant platforms
5. Now maximize all platforms together - p.327

*Advantages:* Wider reach faster, content repurposing efficiency
*Disadvantages:* More work, risk of bad content everywhere - p.331

#### Step 5.2: Maximize Volume
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | Increase posting frequency to accelerate growth |
| **Cognitive Type** | Execution |
| **Automation Potential** | MEDIUM - Scheduling tools help |
| **Executor** | Hybrid |
| **Precondition** | Quality maintained |
| **Output** | Increased content output |
| **Guardrails** | "Quando coloquei dez vezes mais conteudo, minha audiencia cresceu dez vezes mais rapido." - p.77 |
| **Decision Rule** | "Voce so precisa fazer mais, cara." - Influencer mentor, p.76 |

**Volume Targets:**
- Short-form: up to 10x per day per platform - p.319
- Long-form: daily (like soap operas) - p.319
- Podcast example: "duas vezes por semana por quatro anos antes mesmo de ser escolhido na lista Top 100" - p.387

## Part 6: Decision Rules

### DR-001: Topic Selection
```yaml
rule: TOPIC_SELECTION
trigger: Need to create content
condition:
  - IF has_recent_experience THEN use "Passado Recente"
  - IF has_trending_relevance THEN use "Tendencias"
  - IF has_important_lesson THEN use "Passado Distante"
  - IF capturing_live_idea THEN use "Presente"
  - IF willing_to_invest_time THEN use "Fabricado"
action: Select topic category that fits current situation
confidence: HIGH
```

### DR-002: Headline Components
```yaml
rule: HEADLINE_STRENGTH
trigger: Writing headline
condition:
  - IF components >= 2 THEN "STRONG" headline
  - IF components == 1 THEN "ACCEPTABLE" headline
  - IF components == 0 THEN "WEAK" - add components
action: Include at least 2 of 7 components
confidence: HIGH (meta-analysis data)
```

### DR-003: Content Length
```yaml
rule: CONTENT_LENGTH
trigger: Determining content length
condition:
  - IF can_maintain_value_per_second THEN length_is_fine
  - IF boring_sections_exist THEN "TOO_LONG" - cut or improve
action: "Nao existe tal coisa como muito longo, apenas muito chato"
confidence: HIGH
```

### DR-004: Give:Ask Ratio
```yaml
rule: GIVE_ASK_RATIO
trigger: Planning monetization
condition:
  - IF growing_priority THEN give_until_they_ask
  - IF monetizing_priority THEN maintain_minimum_3.5:1
  - IF audience_growth_slowing THEN reduce_asks
action: Prioritize growth over short-term revenue
confidence: HIGH
```

### DR-005: Platform Strategy
```yaml
rule: PLATFORM_SCALING
trigger: Adding new platform
condition:
  - IF current_platform_maximized THEN depth_first_ready
  - IF resources_abundant THEN breadth_first_viable
  - IF single_channel_risk_high THEN prioritize_diversification
action: Choose strategy based on resources and risk tolerance
confidence: MEDIUM
```

### DR-006: Content Quality Validation
```yaml
rule: QUALITY_CHECK
trigger: Content published
condition:
  - IF audience_growing THEN quality_sufficient
  - IF audience_stagnant THEN quality_needs_improvement
  - IF audience_shrinking THEN major_quality_issue
action: "Se nao esta crescendo, suas coisas nao sao tao boas"
confidence: HIGH
```

## Part 7: Exceptions & Edge Cases

### Exception 1: Personal Brand Resistance
**Trigger:** Fear of fame, privacy concerns, hate messages
**Response:** Hormozi's transformation story - p.53-59
**Key Quote:** "Se receber mensagens estranhas e odio de pessoas que nao conheco e o preco que tenho que pagar para causar o impacto que quero ter, eu pagaria esse preco qualquer dia da semana." - p.57

### Exception 2: Competitor Copying
**Trigger:** Others copying your successful content
**Response:** Expected; requires constant innovation - p.93
**Action:** Stay ahead through volume and personal experience

### Exception 3: Content Not Performing
**Trigger:** Individual piece underperforms
**Response:** Normal; not every piece will win
**Action:** "Pratique e voce melhorara." Keep posting, analyze patterns

### Exception 4: Podcast Pre-Scheduling
**Trigger:** Temptation to schedule posts in advance
**Response:** Manual posting performs better
**Rationale:** "Quando voce posta manualmente, sabe que em segundos sera recompensado ou punido pela qualidade do conteudo. Por causa desse loop de feedback proximo, voce tenta muito mais para torna-lo melhor." - p.371

### Exception 5: Audience Already Knows Information
**Trigger:** Worried about repeating yourself
**Response:** "Precisamos Ser Lembrados Mais Do Que Precisamos Ser Ensinados"
**Data:** "Pesquisei minha audiencia e perguntei se sabiam que eu tinha um livro. Um em cada cinco que viu o post disse que nao sabia." - p.361

## Appendix A: The Seven Lessons

### Hormozi's Content Creation Lessons - p.353-371

| # | Lesson | Application |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | "Mude de 'Como fazer' para 'Como eu'" | Speak from experience, not prescription. "Eu faco minha aveia desta forma" vs "Voce deve fazer sua aveia desta forma" - p.355-359 |
| 2 | "Precisamos Ser Lembrados Mais Do Que Precisamos Ser Ensinados" | Repeat yourself. 1 in 5 didn't know about the book despite daily posts - p.361 |
| 3 | "Pocas, Lagoas, Lagos, Oceanos" | Start narrow (local niche), expand over time. King of puddle → pond → lake → ocean - p.363 |
| 4 | "Conteudo Cria Ferramentas Para Vendedores" | Create "greatest hits" list for sales team to use before/after calls - p.365 |
