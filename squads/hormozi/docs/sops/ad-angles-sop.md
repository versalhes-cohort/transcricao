---

## Part 1: Purpose

Build a library of battle-tested ad angles that can be adapted to ANY product/service launch. This SOP provides the specific ANGLES (the conceptual approach), while `goated-ads-sop.md` provides the PROCESS (how to assemble ads) and `hooks-sop.md` provides the TECHNIQUE (how to write hooks).

**Core Insight:**
> "You haven't saturated Facebook. But you have gotten all the low hanging fruit. You've hit a wall with your ad quality, not with the market."

**Why This SOP Exists:**
- Ad angles are the strategic layer between hooks and complete ads
- Same hook template can be used with different angles = exponential variation
- 42+ proven angles from actual $100M book launch = zero guesswork
- Each angle targets different psychological triggers and awareness levels

**The Angle Library Value:**
```
42 Main Angles × 10 Retargeting Angles × 14 Launch Angles × 14 Skool Trial Angles = 80+ Total Angles
Each angle can generate 3-5 hook variations
80 angles × 4 hooks each = 320+ unique ad starting points
```

## Part 5: The Complete Angle Library

### 5.1 RUMORS ARE TRUE ANGLES (6 Angles)

**Master Pattern:**
```
"The rumors are true... [Big claim that defies expectations]"
+ Contrast what people THINK vs. what's ACTUALLY true
+ Real examples (Netflix, Apple, Amazon)
+ Event/offer CTA
```

#### Angle RT-01: Big Companies Secret
**Source:** Ad #38
**Hook:** "The rumors are true... Big companies DO have an unfair advantage. But it's not what you think."
**Core Message:** The advantage is not money, products, or marketing - it's how they THINK about customers.
**Contrast Pattern:**
- Small business: "How do I get someone to buy my thing?"
- Big business: "How do I get someone to keep buying my things?"

**Example Flow:**
```
Small business approach:
• Sell $500 course
• Hope customer is happy
• Maybe they buy again someday

Big business approach:
• Give away $500 course for free
• Sell $200/month implementation program
• Upsell $5,000 done-for-you service
• Keep helping them scale with new offers

Same customer. More value. 10x more revenue.
```

**Adaptation Template:**
```
"The rumors are true... [Industry leaders] DO have [advantage]. But it's not what you think.
It's not [obvious thing 1]. It's not [obvious thing 2]. It's not [obvious thing 3].
The real advantage? [Counterintuitive truth].
[Your avatar] thinks: '[Surface-level question]'
[Industry leaders] think: '[Deeper question]'
[Concrete example showing the difference]"
```

#### Angle RT-02: Rich vs Broke Owners
**Source:** Ad #39
**Hook:** "The rumors are true... Rich business owners ARE different from broke business owners. But not how you think."
**Core Message:** Rich owners make money from CUSTOMERS, broke owners make money from SALES.
**Analogy:** Digging for gold with a spoon vs. heavy machinery.

#### Angle RT-03: Overnight Success
**Source:** Ad #40
**Hook:** "The rumors are true... Some businesses DO become 'overnight successes.' But not how the media tells it."
**Core Message:** They cracked the code on customer economics ($3-5 return per $1 spent vs. $1.20).
**Analogy:** Money printing machine vs. piggy bank.

#### Angle RT-04: Million Dollar Secret
**Source:** Ad #41
**Hook:** "The rumors are true... There IS a million-dollar secret that successful entrepreneurs don't want you to know."
**Core Message:** It's not a product/service/tactic - it's a way of THINKING (transactions vs. relationships).
**Concrete Example:**
```
Transaction: Sell $2,000 website, move on
Relationship: Build $2,000 website → $500/mo hosting → $1,000/mo marketing → $300/mo maintenance → $50,000+ over time
```

#### Angle RT-05: Unfair Advantage
**Source:** Ad #42
**Hook:** "The rumors are true... Some businesses DO have an unfair advantage. And it's 100% legal."
**Core Message:** Customer mathematics (broke math vs. rich math).
**The Math:**
```
Broke Math:
• Spend $100 → Make $120 → $20 profit → Reinvest slowly → Compete on price

Rich Math:
• Spend $100 → Make $400 → $300 profit → Reinvest aggressively → Compete on value
```

#### Angle RT-06: The Original
**Source:** Ad #1
**Hook:** "The rumors are true... There IS a secret that big companies use to crush their competition."
**Examples Used:** Netflix (free trial → monthly), Apple (phone → apps → upgrades), Amazon (free shipping → buy everything).

**Master Pattern:**
```

+ Show the math/logic
+ Event/offer CTA
```

**Source:** Ad #2
**Hook:** "Quick question: What's your email?"

**The Flip:**

```
❌ Better ads
❌ Cheaper leads
❌ More traffic

Real question: "How do I make more money from each customer?"
```

```
Business A: $20 leads → $60 per customer

```

#### Angle QH-03: Is It Possible
**Source:** Ad #4
**Hook:** "Is it possible to 3x your customer value in 90 days? Yes. Here's how:"

```
Single Transaction Thinking: $500 per customer
```

**Analogies:**

#### Angle QH-04: A Lot of People Ask Me
**Source:** Ad #14
**Hook:** "A lot of people ask me what's better: getting more customers or getting more money from customers you already have."

**Source:** Ad #5

**Before/After:**
```

• Run ads → Get customers → Sell $100 → Spend $120 getting them → Lose $20 → Go broke

After:
• Run ads → Get customers → Sell $100 → Immediately sell $200 more → Follow up $160 → Get $50/month → $240 profit day one → Scale like crazy
```

#### Angle TR-02: If I Woke Up Tomorrow
**Source:** Ad #7
**Hook:** "If I woke up with a struggling business tomorrow, and wanted to fix it by December, here's exactly what I would do:"
**90-Day Plan:**
```
Month 1: Figure out what's broken
• How customers find me
• Where they get confused
• Why they don't buy
• How much each customer worth

Month 2: Fix the broken stuff
• Make it easier to understand
• Give more reasons to buy
• Create backup offers
• Build ways to sell more later

Month 3: Test everything
• Try one thing at a time
• Keep good, throw out bad
• More money, less work
```
**Key Insight:** Think like an architect, not a firefighter.


**Source:** Ad #36

```

Step 5: Test and improve
```

#### Angle SF-04: Business Model Theory
**Source:** Ad #9
**Hook:** "Most businesses work like this: Customer buys something. Business hopes they buy again someday. The end."


**Real Examples:**
```

Apple: iPhone gets you in → Apps make phone useful → Cases protect investment → Upgrades keep buying
```

#### Angle SF-05: 12 Week Fix Plan
**Source:** Ad #25
**Hook:** "If you want to fix one thing about your business every week for the next 12 weeks without getting overwhelmed, listen up."
**Weekly Breakdown:**
```

Week 2: Make sure people want it
Week 3: Make it easier to buy
Week 4: Add something else they might want
Week 5: Make saying no harder
Week 6: Get them to come back
Week 7: Get them to buy again
Week 8: Make them tell their friends
Week 9: Keep them happy
Week 10: Find more people like them
Week 11: Do it all faster
Week 12: Do it again
```

#### Angle SF-06: 90% Losing Money
**Source:** Ad #29
**Hook:** "What if I told you that 90% of businesses are losing money on every customer... but they don't know it?"

## Part 7: Exceptions & Edge Cases

### Exception 1: New Product, No Proof
**Trigger:** Launching product with no customer results yet
**Response:**
- Avoid proof-heavy angles (CC-04, SF-03, TR-03)
- Use framework/theory angles (SF-01, SF-02, SF-04)
- Use curiosity angles (RT-01 to RT-06)
- Build proof quickly, then expand angle usage

### Exception 2: Niche-Specific Language
**Trigger:** Avatar uses very specific terminology
**Response:**
- Adapt angle language to niche terms
- Example: "Money model" becomes "patient journey" for healthcare
- Example: "Customer value" becomes "client lifetime" for agencies
- Preserve structure, replace vocabulary

### Exception 3: High-Ticket vs Low-Ticket
**Trigger:** Product is >$5,000 or <$50
**Response:**
```
High-Ticket (>$5,000):
- Emphasize comparison angles (CC-02, CC-08)
- Use transformation proof (TR-03)
- Longer copy, more detail

Low-Ticket (<$50):
- Emphasize trial angles (TF-01 to TF-14)
- Use quick-win angles (TS-01, TS-03)
- Shorter copy, faster CTA
```

### Exception 4: B2B vs B2C
**Trigger:** Different buying psychology
**Response:**
```
B2B:
- Emphasize systems/logic angles (SF-01 to SF-06)
- Use math/economics angles (CC-04, CC-07)
- Focus on ROI language

B2C:
- Emphasize transformation angles (TR-01 to TR-06)
- Use aspiration angles (TR-06, CC-02)
- Focus on lifestyle language
```

### Exception 5: Retargeting Fatigue
**Trigger:** Same audience seeing too many retargeting ads
**Response:**
- Rotate through all 10 retargeting angles weekly
- After 30 days, pause retargeting completely for 7 days
- Return with fresh angles from main library adapted for retargeting
