## Part 1: Purpose

Generate maximum cash in the shortest period possible from your warmest audiences. Fast Cash Plays are "winning lottery tickets you cash in once a quarter" - limited time offers designed to extract premium revenue from existing customers, previous customers, and engaged leads.

**Core Equation:**
```
Fast Cash Revenue = Number Sold x Premium Price
Example:
- 10 customers buy at $6,000 = $60,000
- 90% gross margins = $54,000 profit
- Quarterly execution = $200k+ annual profit boost
```

**10x The 10% Rule:**
```
If 10% of customers pay 10x the price = You double your revenue
- Already spent money to acquire them
- Extra revenue (minus delivery) = straight profit
- Sometimes 2x, 3x, 4x revenue from single customer segment
```

**Business Impact:**
- "Despite Cash Plays only being ~15% of my annual revenue, they were nearly half my profit"
- "Running even one Cash Play per year will pay for itself many years over"
- ROI Example: $300k/year business -> $529k/year with Fast Cash = 76% revenue increase, 4x net income

## Part 2: Scope

### When This SOP Applies
- Business needs immediate cash injection (unexpected expenses, tax bills, investment opportunities)
- Quarterly promotion calendar (recommended: every 90 days)
- Launch of ultra-premium tier for existing offer
- Year-end prepay campaigns

### When This SOP Does NOT Apply
- New customer acquisition (different playbooks)
- Long-term lead nurturing (covered in Lead Nurture SOP)
- Reactivation of dormant leads with no purchase history
- Price reductions or discounting strategies

### Business Types
- B2B and B2C ("this works for both business-to-business offers and business-to-consumer offers")
- Any business with existing customers, previous customers, or engaged leads
- Subscription and one-time purchase models
- Service and product businesses

## Part 5: Process Steps

### PHASE I: PREPARATION

#### Step 1.1: Assemble Target List
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | "Pick and assemble the list you plan to send your offer to (past and present customers)" |
| **Cognitive Type** | Analysis + Segmentation |
| **Automation Potential** | HIGH - CRM query |
| **Executor** | Worker (list pull) + Human (validation) |
| **Precondition** | Contact database with customer history |
| **Output** | Segmented list: existing customers, past customers, engaged leads |
| **Guardrails** | "Exclude Cooled-Off Leads... if your list has neglected leads - aka - you haven't sent them anything for six months or more, consider excluding them" |
| **Decision Rule** | Include only warm audiences with explicit contact permission |

#### Step 1.2: Design Ultra-Premium Offer
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | "Pick the offer you will send. Max out unscalable value. Model the examples." |
| **Cognitive Type** | Synthesis + Creativity |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Strategic decision |
| **Executor** | Human (leadership) |
| **Precondition** | Know what customers find "crazy valuable" |
| **Output** | 1-3 core components + 3 bonuses separated |
| **Guardrails** | "Think of all the most valuable things you can do that are 'unscalable'" |
| **Decision Rule** | "You will want to have one to three core components of your offer, and three bonuses" |

**Offer Structure:**
```
CORE COMPONENTS (1-3):
- Main unscalable value proposition
- Differentiator from standard offer
- Ultimate outcome promise

BONUSES (3, layered in sequence):
- Bonus 1: Added value (Day 5 or Day 4 release)
- Bonus 2: Enhanced feature (Day 2 release)
- Bonus 3: Best bonus - "worth more than entire price tag" (24h release)
```

#### Step 1.3: Set Premium Price
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | "Pick a price to match it. 10x to 50x for maximum cash. Be bold!" |
| **Cognitive Type** | Judgment |
| **Automation Potential** | LOW - Requires market understanding |
| **Executor** | Human |
| **Precondition** | Know current average transaction value |
| **Output** | Premium price point (10x-50x current price) |
| **Guardrails** | "The only thing that should shock them as much as the price is the value that goes with it" |
| **Decision Rule** | "Take whatever your average cart value... and add a zero. And if you want to get frisky, multiply it by five" |

**Pricing Examples:**
| Business Type | Standard Price | Fast Cash Price | Multiple |
|---------------|----------------|-----------------|----------|
| Fitness B2C | $150/month | $10,000/year | ~56x monthly |
| Marketing Agency B2B | ~$5,000/year | $50,000/year | 10x |
| Subscription | Monthly price | Monthly x 12 | 12x |

#### Step 1.4: Set Quantity Cap
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Description** | "We want to cap how many people can buy it" |
| **Cognitive Type** | Analysis |
| **Automation Potential** | MEDIUM - Calculation |
| **Executor** | Hybrid |
| **Precondition** | Know customer count and fulfillment capacity |
| **Output** | Limited spots number (5-10% of customers) |
| **Guardrails** | "Cap it to a number where you will absolutely sell out" |
| **Decision Rule** | "If you had 100 customers, you'd limit it to 5-10. If you have 1000 or more, then fulfillment may constrain you" |

#### Step 1.5: Choose Selling Method

## Part 6: Decision Rules

### DR-001: Audience Selection
```yaml
rule: AUDIENCE_INCLUSION
trigger: Building Fast Cash list
condition:
  - IF customer_status == "current" THEN INCLUDE
  - IF customer_status == "past_good_standing" THEN INCLUDE
  - IF lead_status == "engaged" AND has_permission THEN INCLUDE
  - IF no_contact_6_months == true THEN EXCLUDE
action: "Exclude Cooled-Off Leads... consider excluding them from this offer but start warming them up for the next one"
confidence: HIGH (source: Important Points, p.13)
```

### DR-002: Price Setting
```yaml
rule: PREMIUM_PRICING
trigger: Setting Fast Cash price
condition:
  - IF goal == "max_cash" THEN price = avg_cart_value * 10 TO 50
  - IF goal == "volume" THEN price = lower_multiple + scale_features
action: "Take whatever your average cart value... and add a zero. And if you want to get frisky, multiply it by five"
confidence: HIGH (source: What to charge, p.8)
```

### DR-003: Quantity Cap
```yaml
rule: SCARCITY_LIMIT
trigger: Setting Fast Cash quantity
condition:
  - IF customer_count < 100 THEN limit = "handful" (private, personal appeal)
  - IF customer_count >= 100 THEN limit = 5-10% of customers
  - IF fulfillment_constrains THEN limit = fulfillment_capacity
action: "Cap it to a number where you will absolutely sell out"
confidence: HIGH (source: How many to sell, p.8)
```

### DR-004: Selling Method Selection
```yaml
rule: SALES_PATH
trigger: Choosing consult vs auto checkout
condition:
  - IF price == "high" AND sales_team_capacity == true THEN use_consult_path
  - IF price == "high" AND brand_strength == "very_strong" THEN allow_auto_checkout
  - IF sales_team == "limited" THEN spread_consults_over_7_days
action: "For most businesses, high price points deserve a one-on-one consultation"
confidence: HIGH (source: How to sell it, p.8)
```

### DR-005: Bonus Deployment
```yaml
rule: BONUS_TIMING
trigger: Releasing bonuses in sequence
condition:
  - Bonus 1: Release Day 5 (consult) or Day 2 (auto) - "first additional bonus"
  - Bonus 2: Release Day 2 (consult) or +6h (auto) - "second bonus"
  - Bonus 3: Release 24h out (consult) or cart open (auto) - "best bonus"
action: "Layer in these bonuses one at a time to increase the likelihood people buy"
confidence: HIGH (source: Description, p.7-8)
```

### DR-006: Quarterly Cadence
```yaml
rule: FAST_CASH_FREQUENCY
trigger: Scheduling next Fast Cash Play
condition:
  - IF quarter_elapsed THEN schedule_next_fast_cash
  - IF list_fatigue_concerns THEN extend_to_6_months
action: "Once a quarter is the sweet spot"
confidence: HIGH (source: 5 Reasons, p.15)
```

## Part 7: Exceptions & Edge Cases

### Exception 1: Not Selling Out Fast Enough
**Trigger:** "If you haven't sold out, or at least a substantial percentage" by midpoint
**Action:** "Consider adding things to the offer to make it more compelling before sending the remaining emails and texts"
**Source:** p.19 Sample Email section

### Exception 2: Too Many Bad Leads on List
**Trigger:** Cold leads who haven't been contacted in 6+ months
**Action:** "Consider excluding them from this offer but start warming them up for the next one. For now, just stick with existing customers and past customers in good standing."
**Source:** p.13 Important Points

### Exception 3: Limited Sales Staff
**Trigger:** Cannot handle all consults in compressed timeframe
**Action:** "Make your thing available for purchase day one, but drive people to book appointments. This way you can spread out the consults and see everyone, even with a limited sales staff (which is sometimes just you)"
**Source:** p.8 How to sell it

### Exception 4: No Cards on File
**Trigger:** Need to collect payment but don't have card info
**Action:** "Just call them up to collect their card immediately after they respond"
**Source:** p.8 How to sell it

### Exception 5: Customer Backs Out After Purchase
**Trigger:** Paid customer cancels
**Action:** Send "one spot left" notification to waitlist: "If they had payment ready and just got there a little too late, get permission to notify them if a spot opens up - and charge them if it does"
**Source:** p.10-11 Both sequences

### Exception 6: Over-Demand (Waitlist Management)
**Trigger:** More buyers than spots
**Action:** "I'd rather you sell out and then waitlist people who already tried to buy"
**Source:** p.10 Note
