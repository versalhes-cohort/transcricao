## Purpose
Design a brand identity that supports premium positioning and long-term asset value.

> "A brand is not a logo. A brand is what people say about you when you're not in the room." — Alex Hormozi

---

## Brand Philosophy

### Brand ≠ Marketing
- Marketing: Getting people to buy
- Brand: Getting people to pay MORE and STAY LONGER

### The Long Game
Brand is an investment:
- Short-term: Costs money, doesn't show ROI
- Long-term: Premium pricing, lower CAC, higher LTV

### When Brand Matters Most
- Crowded market
- Commodity products
- High-price points
- Long customer relationships

---

## Brand Building Blocks

### 1. Brand Promise
The transformation you guarantee.
```
"We help [AVATAR] achieve [RESULT] through [METHOD]."
```

### 2. Brand Values
What you stand for (and against).
- 3-5 core values
- With specific behaviors for each
- What you will/won't do

### 3. Brand Voice
How you communicate.
- Tone (formal, casual, bold, gentle)
- Vocabulary (industry jargon, simple, technical)
- Personality (serious, playful, authoritative)

### 4. Brand Story
Why you exist.
- Origin story
- Mission
- Vision for the future

### 5. Visual Identity
How you look.
- Logo
- Colors
- Typography
- Imagery style

## Execution Steps

### Step 1: Define Brand Promise
What transformation do you provide?

### Step 2: Identify Values
What do you believe that others don't?

### Step 3: Develop Voice Guidelines
How should all communication sound?

### Step 4: Write Brand Story
Where did you come from? Where are you going?

### Step 5: Create Visual Guidelines
What does your brand look like?

### Step 6: Document Everything
Create brand guidelines others can follow.

## Brand Voice Guidelines Template

```yaml
brand_voice:
  tone:
    we_are: ["direct", "confident", "educational"]
    we_are_not: ["aggressive", "arrogant", "salesy"]

  vocabulary:
    always_use: ["evidence", "framework", "system", "result"]
    never_use: ["hustle", "grind", "guru", "secrets"]

  personality:
    traits: ["authoritative", "data-driven", "slightly provocative"]
    example_phrases:
      - "The data shows..."
      - "Most people think X. They're wrong."
      - "Here's the framework..."
```

## Brand Consistency Checklist

- [ ] Website matches brand voice
- [ ] Social media follows visual guidelines

- [ ] Sales scripts align with brand values
- [ ] Customer support sounds like brand
- [ ] All team members trained on guidelines

---

## Common Brand Mistakes

1. **Changing too often** — Consistency builds recognition
2. **Copying competitors** — Differentiation is the point
3. **All style, no substance** — Brand follows results, not vice versa
4. **Ignoring customer language** — Speak how they speak
5. **Over-designing** — Simple is memorable
