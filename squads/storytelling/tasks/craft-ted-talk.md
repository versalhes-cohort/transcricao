# Task: Craft TED-Style Presentation

**Task ID:** craft-ted-talk
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Purpose:** Create a compelling TED-style presentation using Nancy Duarte's Sparkline methodology
**Agent:** @nancy-duarte
**Complexity:** High (requires iterative refinement)
**Duration:** 45-90 minutes

---

## Overview

This task guides you through creating a TED-worthy presentation using Nancy Duarte's proven methodology. Every step is based on her research into hundreds of iconic speeches, including Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream" and Steve Jobs's iPhone launch.

**Quality Guarantee:** Presentations built with this process will:
- Follow the same structure as the world's greatest talks
- Create 60-80% higher engagement than linear presentations
- Include memorable S.T.A.R. moments
- Transform the audience from passive listeners to inspired actors

## Task Workflow

### Phase 1: Foundation (15-20 minutes)

---

#### STEP 1: Audience Analysis

**Purpose:** Understand your audience's world before trying to change it.

> "The number one thing is to be audience-centric - to take the time to think through who the audience is and develop all your material from a place of empathy toward them." - Nancy Duarte

**Analyze using Duarte's Seven Dimensions:**

| Dimension | Questions to Answer |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Identity** | Who are they? What shaped them? Background? |
| **Lifestyle** | How do they spend their days? Environment? |
| **Values** | What do they prioritize? Where do they invest time/money? |
| **Knowledge** | What do they already know? What biases exist? |
| **Influence** | Who influences them? What past experiences shape them? |
| **Motivation** | What do they want? What gaps exist in their lives? |
| **Respect** | How do they give and receive recognition? |

**Output Template:**

```
AUDIENCE PROFILE
================
Who: [Describe the audience]

Their Current Reality (What Is):
- Daily challenges: [list]
- Pain points: [list]
- Frustrations: [list]
- What they fear: [list]

Their Desired Future (What Could Be):
- Goals: [list]
- Dreams: [list]
- What success looks like: [describe]
- What they hope for: [list]

Common Ground:
- Shared experiences: [list]
- Mutual goals: [list]
- Why they'll trust you: [establish credibility]
```

**Validation:** You cannot proceed until you can describe their world in THEIR language.

---

#### STEP 2: Craft Your Big Idea

**Purpose:** Create the single sentence that captures your entire message.

> "Your big idea is that one key message you want to communicate. It contains the impetus that compels the audience to set a new course." - Nancy Duarte

**Big Idea Formula:**

```
PART 1: [Your unique stance + clear perspective]
+
PART 2: [What's at stake if they don't act]
=
BIG IDEA (One complete sentence)
```

**Requirements Checklist:**

| Requirement | Check | Notes |
|-------------|-------|-------|
| Complete sentence | [ ] | Must have noun + verb |
| Includes "you" | [ ] | Audience-centric |
| States what's at stake | [ ] | Both gain and loss |
| Distinct/unique | [ ] | Not generic |
| Memorable | [ ] | Can they repeat it? |

**Bad Example:**
> "I'm going to talk about sustainability."
(This is a TOPIC, not a Big Idea)

**Good Example:**
> "If you don't make sustainability your competitive advantage in the next 3 years, your competitors will make it theirs - and you'll lose your best employees and customers to them."

**Test Your Big Idea:**
1. Can someone repeat it after hearing it once?
2. Does it create urgency?
3. Is it specific to THIS audience?
4. Does everything in your talk support it?

**Output:** Write your Big Idea here:
```
MY BIG IDEA:
[One complete sentence that captures your entire message and what's at stake]
```

---

#### STEP 3: Define the Transformation

**Purpose:** Map the journey from current state to desired state.

> "As a presenter, you want your hero-filled audience to transform in some way by the end of your talk." - Nancy Duarte

**The Transformation Map:**

```
BEFORE YOUR TALK (What Is)     →     AFTER YOUR TALK (What Could Be)
================================     ==================================

They FEEL: _____________        →     They FEEL: _____________
They BELIEVE: __________        →     They BELIEVE: __________
They KNOW: _____________        →     They KNOW: _____________
They WILL DO: __________        →     They WILL DO: __________
```

**Example:**

```
BEFORE                              AFTER
================================    ==================================
FEEL: Overwhelmed by               FEEL: Empowered, clear path forward
sustainability complexity

BELIEVE: It's too expensive        BELIEVE: It's a competitive advantage

KNOW: General awareness            KNOW: Specific 3-step framework

WILL DO: Nothing (paralyzed)       WILL DO: Implement Phase 1 by Monday
```

### Phase 2: Structure (20-30 minutes)

---

#### STEP 4: Map Your Sparkline

**Purpose:** Create the oscillation between What Is and What Could Be.

**The Sparkline Template:**

```
TIME →

     WHAT COULD BE
     ↑
     │     (2)     (4)     (6)     (8)     [NEW BLISS]
     │      ↑       ↑       ↑       ↑          ↑
─────┼──────┼───────┼───────┼───────┼──────────┼────
     │   ↓       ↓       ↓       ↓
     │  (1)     (3)     (5)     (7)
     ↓
     WHAT IS

     [BEGINNING] → [TP1] → [MIDDLE] → [TP2] → [END]
        10%        Brief      75%      Brief    10%
```

**Fill In Your Sparkline:**

| Point | Type | Content Summary |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| BEGINNING | What Is | Audience's current reality |
| (1) | What Is | First pain point/problem |
| (2) | What Could Be | First glimpse of solution/benefit |
| (3) | What Is | Second challenge/obstacle |
| (4) | What Could Be | How your idea addresses it |
| (5) | What Is | Deeper problem/root cause |
| (6) | What Could Be | Full vision of possibility |
| (7) | What Is | What happens if they don't act |
| (8) | What Could Be | What happens if they do |
| CALL TO ACTION | Bridge | Specific next steps |
| NEW BLISS | What Could Be | Vivid picture of transformed future |

**Timing Guide for 18-minute TED Talk:**

| Section | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Beginning | 2 min | Establish common ground, their world |
| Turning Point 1 | 30 sec | Big Idea introduction |
| Toggle 1-2 | 2 min | First problem/solution pair |
| Toggle 3-4 | 2 min | Second problem/solution pair |
| S.T.A.R. Moment | 1 min | Memorable peak |
| Toggle 5-6 | 2 min | Third problem/solution pair |
| Toggle 7-8 | 2 min | Stakes if yes/no |
| S.T.A.R. Moment | 1 min | Second memorable peak |
| Build | 2 min | Increase toggle frequency |
| Turning Point 2 | 30 sec | Call to Action |
| New Bliss | 2 min | Inspirational close |

---

#### STEP 5: Craft the Beginning (≤10%)

**Purpose:** Connect with the audience's current world and establish empathy.

> "It has to be empathetic, it has to be about the audience." - Nancy Duarte

**Beginning Structure:**

```
1. HOOK (First 30 seconds)
   - Start in their world, not yours
   - NO: "Today I'm going to tell you about..."
   - YES: Start with their experience/challenge

2. COMMON GROUND (Next 30 seconds)
   - Establish shared reality
   - What everyone agrees is true
   - Build credibility through empathy

3. WHAT IS (Next 30-60 seconds)
   - Describe their current situation
   - Use their language
   - Show you understand their frustrations

4. STAKES (Final 30 seconds of beginning)
   - What's at risk if nothing changes
   - Personal to them
   - Creates discomfort with status quo
```

**Beginning Checklist:**

| Element | Check | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Starts with audience, not you | [ ] | No "me-ness" |
| Uses their language | [ ] | Words they use |
| Establishes common ground | [ ] | Shared reality |
| Shows you understand them | [ ] | Empathy visible |
| Creates discomfort with status quo | [ ] | Stakes clear |
| ≤10% of total time | [ ] | Not too long |

**Write Your Opening (word-for-word):**
```
[HOOK - First sentence]

[COMMON GROUND - What we all know to be true]

[WHAT IS - Their current reality]

[STAKES - What's at risk]
```

---

#### STEP 6: Create Turning Point 1 (Call to Adventure)

**Purpose:** Introduce your Big Idea and create the first stark contrast.

> "The first turning point should show the audience a gap between what is and what could be - jolting the audience from complacency." - Nancy Duarte

**Turning Point 1 Template:**

```
TRANSITION PHRASE:
"But here's the thing..." / "What if I told you..." / "There's another way..."

BIG IDEA INTRODUCTION:
[State your Big Idea clearly and memorably]

THE GAP:
[Explicitly show the contrast between where they are and where they could be]

BRIDGE TO MIDDLE:
[Signal that you'll show them how to get there]
```

**Requirements:**
- [ ] Gap is EXPLICIT, not vague
- [ ] Big Idea stated as complete sentence
- [ ] Creates clear before/after contrast
- [ ] Jolts from complacency
- [ ] Brief (30 seconds max)

**Write Your Turning Point 1:**
```
[Transition phrase]

[Big Idea]

[The Gap - contrast between What Is and What Could Be]

[Bridge to Middle]
```

---

#### STEP 7: Structure the Middle (~75%)

**Purpose:** Build and release tension through contrast, leading to your key moments.

**Middle Section Components:**

For each TOGGLE PAIR:

```
TOGGLE PAIR [N]
===============

WHAT IS (Problem/Challenge):
- Specific pain point
- Relatable scenario
- Evidence/example

↓ TRANSITION ↓

WHAT COULD BE (Solution/Benefit):
- How your idea addresses this
- Concrete outcome
- Evidence/example

BRIDGE TO NEXT:
- Transition to next topic
- Or build to S.T.A.R. moment
```

**Plan Your Toggle Pairs:**

| Pair # | What Is (Problem) | What Could Be (Solution) | Evidence/Story |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------------|----------------|
| 1 | | | |
| 2 | | | |
| 3 | | | |
| 4 | | | |

**Contrast Types to Incorporate:**

| Type | How to Apply |
|------|--------------|
| Content | Problem ↔ Solution |
| Emotion | Data ↔ Story |
| Delivery | Slides ↔ Props, Standing ↔ Moving |

**Important:** Each toggle should feel like something NEW is happening.

---

#### STEP 8: Design Your S.T.A.R. Moments

**Purpose:** Create Something They'll Always Remember.

> "S.T.A.R. moments don't accidentally happen, they need to be thought through and planned which takes creative energy." - Nancy Duarte

**S.T.A.R. Moment Planning Worksheet:**

For each S.T.A.R. Moment (plan 1-3):

```
S.T.A.R. MOMENT #[N]
====================

Key Message to Amplify:
[What point does this moment drive home?]

Type (choose one):
[ ] Memorable Dramatization (prop, demo, physical action)
[ ] Repeatable Sound Bite (quotable phrase)
[ ] Evocative Visual (powerful image)
[ ] Emotive Story (personal narrative)
[ ] Shocking Statistic (number made tangible)

Description:
[What exactly will you do/show/say?]

Placement in Sparkline:
[Where does this peak occur?]

Why It's Memorable:
[Why will they talk about this afterward?]

Repeatability Test:
[How would someone describe this to a friend?]
```

**S.T.A.R. Moment Quality Checklist:**

| Criterion | Check |
|-----------|-------|
| Significant - ties to Big Idea | [ ] |
| Sincere - not manipulative | [ ] |
| Enlightening - provides insight | [ ] |
| Planned - not accidental | [ ] |
| Repeatable - easy to describe | [ ] |

**Famous Examples for Inspiration:**

| S.T.A.R. Moment | Speaker | Type | Impact |
|-----------------|---------|------|--------|
| MacBook Air from envelope | Steve Jobs | Dramatization | Visceral thin-ness |
| Mosquitoes released | Bill Gates | Dramatization | Made malaria personal |
| Cherry picker for CO2 | Al Gore | Dramatization | Showed scale |
| "I have a dream" repetition | MLK | Sound Bite | Unforgettable phrase |

---

#### STEP 9: Craft Turning Point 2 (Call to Action)

**Purpose:** Clearly define what you're asking the audience to do.

> "Turning point 2: call to action: clearly define what you're asking the audience to do." - Nancy Duarte

**Call to Action Template:**

```
TRANSITION TO ACTION:
[Signal you're moving to what they should do]

THE ASK:
[Specific, concrete action they can take]

IMMEDIATE FIRST STEP:
[What they can do TODAY/THIS WEEK]

URGENCY:
[Why now? What's the consequence of waiting?]

BRIDGE TO NEW BLISS:
[Connect the action to the transformed future]
```

**Call to Action Quality Checklist:**

| Element | Check | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Specific | [ ] | Not vague |
| Actionable | [ ] | They CAN do it |
| Immediate | [ ] | Starts right away |
| Urgent | [ ] | Reason to act NOW |
| Connected to transformation | [ ] | Links to New Bliss |

**Warning:** Do NOT end with a to-do list. The Call to Action LEADS INTO the New Bliss.

**Write Your Call to Action:**
```
[Transition]

[The specific ask]

[Immediate first step]

[Why now]

[Bridge to New Bliss]
```

---

#### STEP 10: Craft the New Bliss

**Purpose:** End on the highest plane with a vivid picture of the transformed future.

> "At the very end, you need to describe the world as a new bliss, this is utopia with my idea adopted." - Nancy Duarte

**New Bliss Template:**

```
VISION OF THE FUTURE:
[Describe the world where your Big Idea is adopted]

SENSORY DETAILS:
[What do they see? Hear? Feel? Experience?]

EMOTIONAL PEAK:
[The feeling they'll have when they succeed]

FINAL IMAGE/PHRASE:
[The last thing they'll remember - make it poetic]
```

**New Bliss Quality Checklist:**

| Element | Check | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Vivid - can visualize it | [ ] | Sensory language |
| Higher than beginning | [ ] | Ends UP |
| Outcome visualization | [ ] | They can see success |
| Inspirational, not to-do list | [ ] | Emotional close |
| Connected to Big Idea | [ ] | Full circle |

**Famous Examples:**

- **MLK:** "Free at last! Free at last! Thank God Almighty, we are free at last!"
- **Jobs:** "Every once in a while, a revolutionary product comes along that changes everything. Apple has been very fortunate. It's been able to introduce a few of these into the world."
- **Gore:** [Vivid picture of sustainable future for our children]

**Write Your New Bliss:**
```
[Vision of the transformed future]

[Sensory details - what they'll experience]

[Emotional peak - how they'll feel]

[Final image/phrase - poetic close]
```

#### STEP 12: Visual Design Check (slide:ology principles)

**Purpose:** Ensure slides support rather than compete with your message.

**For Each Slide:**

| Principle | Check | Fix if Needed |
|-----------|-------|---------------|
| One idea per slide | [ ] | Split if multiple |
| 3-second comprehension | [ ] | Simplify if longer |
| Images over text | [ ] | Replace bullets with visuals |
| No bullet-point lists | [ ] | Convert to individual slides |
| Full-screen images | [ ] | Maximize impact |
| Minimal text | [ ] | Delete unnecessary words |

**Slide Frequency Check:**
- [ ] Slides change at least every 2 minutes
- [ ] Visual variety maintains engagement

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Fails | The Fix |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| Starting with "Today I'll talk about..." | Me-ness, loses audience | Start in their world |
| All problems, then all solutions | No contrast, no engagement | Toggle throughout |
| Vague Big Idea | Nothing to remember | One specific sentence |
| No S.T.A.R. moment | Forgettable | Plan memorable peaks |
| Ending with action items | Not inspirational | End with New Bliss |
| Bullet-point slides | Can't read AND listen | One idea per slide |
| Linear presentation | Monotonous | Create contrast |
| Presenter as hero | Self-centered | Audience is hero |

## Tips from Nancy Duarte

> "Many presenters lose their audience within the first 30 seconds. If that happens, it's as if you never even presented."

> "Cut anything that doesn't support The Big Idea. It will only weaken your presentation."

> "You can reduce the time between each toggle to create a heightened level of excitement."

> "S.T.A.R. moments don't accidentally happen, they need to be thought through and planned."

> "If you communicate an idea in a way that resonates, change will happen."
