## Overview

This task guides professionals through applying Park Howell's ABT (And, But, Therefore) Framework to any communication challenge. Based on the methodology developed by Dr. Randy Olson and refined for business by Park Howell.

> "You must master the ABT to build your narrative intuition. Set up/problem/resolution - the three-act story structure that our cause-and-effect, pattern-seeking, loop-closing brain craves." - Park Howell

**What This Task Produces:**
1. Core ABT statement (15-second version)
2. Expanded ABT with supporting details
3. Application-specific versions (email, pitch, presentation)
4. Five Primal Elements story to support the ABT
5. Story Cycle alignment check
6. Validation against compelling clarity criteria

**Sources:**

- "Brand Bewitchery" - Park Howell, 2020 (PRIMARY)

## Phase 1: Understand the Communication Challenge

### Step 1.1: Define the Communication Context

**Instruction:** Understand what you're trying to communicate and why.

**Elicitation Questions:**
1. "What is the core message you need to communicate?"
2. "Who is your audience and what do they care about?"
3. "What action do you want them to take after hearing this?"
4. "What format will this take? (email, pitch, presentation, social post, etc.)"

**Template:**
```yaml
communication_context:
  core_message: "[What you need to say]"
  audience:
    who: "[Specific audience description]"
    care_about: "[Their priorities and concerns]"
    current_belief: "[What they think now]"
  desired_action: "[What you want them to do]"
  format: "[Email / Pitch / Presentation / Social / Speech / Other]"
  timing: "[When and where will this be delivered]"
```

### Step 1.2: Identify the Narrative Problem

**Instruction:** Diagnose why current communication isn't working.

**Common Narrative Problems:**

| Problem | Symptom | ABT Solution |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Too much "And"** | Lists facts without connection | Add "But" to create tension |
| **No clear conflict** | Audience doesn't engage | Find the real problem/tension |
| **Unclear resolution** | No call to action | Define the "Therefore" |
| **Feature-focused** | Talks about product, not outcome | Shift to customer transformation |
| **No hook** | Loses audience immediately | Lead with compelling "And, But" |

**Diagnostic Questions:**
- Does your current message have a clear problem statement?
- Is there tension that demands resolution?
- Does the audience know what to do next?
- Would someone lean in and say "tell me more"?

**Template:**
```yaml
narrative_diagnosis:
  current_state: "[What you have now]"
  primary_problem: "[Main issue with current narrative]"
  missing_element: "[And / But / Therefore]"
  fix_focus: "[What needs to be added/changed]"
```

## Phase 2: Build the Core ABT

### Step 2.1: Identify the "AND" (Agreement/Context)

**Instruction:** Establish shared context and raise stakes.

> "The 'And' sets up agreement. It's Act One - setting the scene and establishing what your protagonist wants in the world."

**Purpose of "AND":**
- Establish common ground with audience
- Show you understand their world
- Raise the stakes of what matters
- Create the baseline for tension

**Questions to Answer:**
1. What situation does your audience agree is true?
2. What do they want or aspire to?
3. What context is relevant to your message?
4. What stakes make this matter to them?

**Structure Options:**

**Option A: Situation + Aspiration**
```
[Audience] wants [outcome] AND [why it matters]...
```

**Option B: Trend + Opportunity**
```
[Industry trend] is happening AND [opportunity it creates]...
```

**Option C: Shared Belief + Raised Stakes**
```
We all agree [belief] AND [what's at stake]...
```

**Template:**
```yaml
and_component:
  shared_reality: "[What you both agree is true]"
  raised_stakes: "[Why it matters / what they want]"
  full_statement: "[Complete 'And' phrase]"

  # Validation
  validates_agreement: "[Yes/No - do they agree with this?]"
  raises_stakes: "[Yes/No - does it matter to them?]"
```

**Examples:**

| Context | AND Statement |
|---------|---------------|
| SaaS Product | "Companies need to manage content AND developers want proven solutions" |
| Job Application | "Your team is growing fast AND you need people who can hit the ground running" |
| Sales Pitch | "You're looking to increase revenue AND you know customer experience drives growth" |
| Internal Proposal | "Our department handles critical work AND leadership expects measurable results" |

---

### Step 2.2: Identify the "BUT" (Conflict/Problem)

**Instruction:** Introduce the tension that demands resolution.

> "The word 'but' is particularly powerful because it changes the direction of a discussion and introduces conflict, which creates tension and curiosity."

**Purpose of "BUT":**
- Jolts audience out of autopilot
- Activates problem-solving brain
- Creates anticipation for resolution
- Differentiates you (shows you understand the problem)

**Questions to Answer:**
1. What problem prevents them from getting what they want?
2. What tension exists in their current situation?
3. What's the obstacle, challenge, or barrier?
4. What keeps them up at night?

**Types of "BUT":**

| Type | Example | When to Use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Problem** | "...BUT most solutions are unreliable" | When there's a clear obstacle |
| **Contradiction** | "...BUT current approaches don't work" | When status quo is failing |
| **Tension** | "...BUT resources are limited" | When competing priorities exist |
| **Challenge** | "...BUT the market is changing fast" | When external pressure exists |
| **Gap** | "...BUT there's a disconnect between..." | When misalignment exists |

**Structure Options:**

**Option A: Direct Problem**
```
BUT [the specific problem preventing success]...
```

**Option B: Contradiction**
```
BUT [what should work] isn't working / doesn't exist...
```

**Option C: External Challenge**
```
BUT [external force] is making this difficult...
```

**Template:**
```yaml
but_component:
  core_problem: "[The main obstacle/tension]"
  why_it_matters: "[Impact on the audience]"
  full_statement: "[Complete 'But' phrase]"

  # Validation
  creates_tension: "[Yes/No - does it create 'what now?' feeling?]"
  is_real: "[Yes/No - is this a genuine problem they recognize?]"
  demands_resolution: "[Yes/No - does it need to be solved?]"
```

**Examples:**

| Context | BUT Statement |
|---------|---------------|
| SaaS Product | "BUT building custom solutions causes delays and budget overruns" |
| Job Application | "BUT experienced candidates often need extensive onboarding" |
| Sales Pitch | "BUT traditional approaches create friction and slow down deals" |
| Internal Proposal | "BUT our current tools don't give us visibility into performance" |

**"BUT" Variations:**
Sometimes "But" can be replaced with:
- However
- Yet
- Unfortunately
- The challenge is
- The problem is
- What's missing is

---

### Step 2.3: Identify the "THEREFORE" (Resolution/Action)

**Instruction:** Deliver the resolution that addresses the tension.

> "The 'Therefore' is the resolution - what changes, what action follows, what you're proposing. It must logically follow from the And and But."

**Purpose of "THEREFORE":**
- Resolves the tension created by "But"
- Provides clear direction or action
- Differentiates your solution
- Moves the audience to act

**Questions to Answer:**
1. What solves the problem you identified?
2. What action should the audience take?
3. What's your unique solution or approach?
4. What changes if they accept your proposal?

**Structure Options:**

**Option A: Solution Statement**
```
THEREFORE [your solution/approach]...
```

**Option B: Call to Action**
```
THEREFORE [what they should do]...
```

**Option C: Outcome Promise**
```
THEREFORE [what will be different]...
```

**Template:**
```yaml
therefore_component:
  resolution: "[What solves the problem]"
  action_required: "[What the audience should do]"
  full_statement: "[Complete 'Therefore' phrase]"

  # Validation
  resolves_tension: "[Yes/No - does it address the 'But'?]"
  is_actionable: "[Yes/No - is there a clear next step?]"
  differentiates: "[Yes/No - is it unique to you?]"
```

**Examples:**

| Context | THEREFORE Statement |
|---------|---------------------|
| SaaS Product | "THEREFORE we provide enterprise-ready components that deploy in hours, not months" |
| Job Application | "THEREFORE I bring both the experience you need AND the ability to contribute immediately" |
| Sales Pitch | "THEREFORE our platform streamlines the process while improving customer experience" |
| Internal Proposal | "THEREFORE I'm proposing we implement [solution] to achieve [outcome]" |

### Step 2.4: Assemble the Complete ABT

**Instruction:** Combine all three components into a single, flowing statement.

**The ABT Formula:**
```
[AND: Context + Stakes],
[BUT: Problem/Tension],
[THEREFORE: Resolution/Action].
```

**Guidelines for Assembly:**
1. **Flow naturally** - Should sound like spoken language
2. **Stay concise** - Target 15-30 seconds when spoken
3. **Use transitions** - "And," "But," "Therefore" should connect smoothly
4. **End strong** - The "Therefore" is your landing point

**Template:**
```yaml
complete_abt:
  full_statement: |
    [Write the complete ABT here, flowing naturally]

  spoken_time: "[Target: 15-30 seconds]"

  # Quality Check
  flows_naturally: "[Yes/No]"
  can_be_memorized: "[Yes/No]"
  makes_audience_lean_in: "[Yes/No]"
```

**Assembly Examples:**

**SaaS Product:**
```
Companies need reliable content editing AND developers want proven,
enterprise-ready solutions,
BUT building custom editors causes delays and budget overruns,
THEREFORE TinyMCE provides battle-tested components that deploy
in hours instead of months.
```

**Job Application:**
```
Your team is scaling quickly AND you need experienced people who
can contribute immediately,
BUT most candidates either lack experience OR require extensive onboarding,
THEREFORE I bring five years of directly relevant experience
plus a track record of ramping up fast.
```

**Sales Pitch:**
```
You're looking to increase revenue AND you know customer experience
drives growth,
BUT traditional sales approaches create friction that slows deals,
THEREFORE our platform streamlines your sales process while
improving customer satisfaction scores.
```

**Internal Proposal:**
```
Our department delivers critical work AND leadership expects
measurable results,
BUT our current tools don't give us visibility into performance,
THEREFORE I'm proposing we implement [Solution] to achieve
30% improvement in tracking accuracy.
```

## Phase 3: ABT Validation

### Step 3.1: Apply the Lean-In Test

**Instruction:** Verify your ABT creates engagement.

**The Lean-In Test:**
> "When you deliver your ABT, does the audience lean forward and say 'Tell me more'?"

**Validation Checklist:**

| Criterion | Question | Pass/Fail |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Hook** | Does it grab attention in the first 5 seconds? | |
| **Relevance** | Does it address what the audience cares about? | |
| **Tension** | Does the "But" create genuine tension? | |
| **Resolution** | Does the "Therefore" resolve the tension? | |
| **Action** | Is there a clear next step implied or stated? | |
| **Memorability** | Can you remember and repeat it? | |
| **Speakability** | Does it sound natural when spoken aloud? | |

**Red Flags:**
- [ ] "And" goes on too long (more than 2 clauses)
- [ ] "But" is weak or artificial tension
- [ ] "Therefore" doesn't connect to the "But"
- [ ] Takes more than 30 seconds to deliver
- [ ] Uses jargon the audience won't understand
- [ ] Makes you the hero instead of the mentor

### Step 3.2: Check for AAA Contamination

**Instruction:** Ensure your ABT hasn't slipped into "And, And, And" territory.

**AAA Warning Signs:**
```
PROBLEMATIC: "We offer great service AND competitive pricing
AND fast delivery AND excellent support AND..."
```

**This is listing features without narrative tension. It needs a "But" to create engagement.**

**AAA vs ABT Comparison:**

| Version | Example | Problem |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **AAA (Bad)** | "We have great products AND excellent service AND fast shipping AND..." | List without tension |
| **ABT (Good)** | "Customers want quality AND fast delivery, BUT most suppliers make you choose, THEREFORE we deliver both" | Narrative with tension |

**Fix AAA by asking:**
1. What PROBLEM do all these features solve?
2. What TENSION exists in the market?
3. What would be MISSING without you?

## Phase 4: Expand and Apply

### Step 4.1: Create Application-Specific Versions

**Instruction:** Adapt your core ABT for different contexts.

#### Email Opening

**Purpose:** Hook the recipient and get them to read the full email.

**Template:**
```
Subject: [Compelling subject based on "But" tension]

[Name],

[And - shared context, 1 sentence]
[But - the problem, 1 sentence]
[Therefore - why you're reaching out, 1 sentence]

[Rest of email...]
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: The content editing challenge [Company] is facing

Hi Sarah,

I noticed [Company] is scaling your content team. Companies in
rapid growth need consistent, reliable editing tools, BUT building
custom solutions pulls engineering from core products.

That's why I wanted to share how [Solution] helped similar
companies deploy in days instead of months.

[Continue...]
```

---

#### Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)

**Purpose:** Deliver your value proposition in a brief encounter.

**Template:**
```
[And - Who you help and what they want]
[But - The challenge they face]
[Therefore - How you solve it differently]
[Hook - Offer to share more]
```

**Example:**
```
"I help SaaS companies build better products faster. Most teams
want enterprise-grade features, BUT building them from scratch
takes months and pulls focus from core innovation. THEREFORE
I provide ready-to-deploy components that teams can implement
in hours. Would you like to see some examples?"
```

#### Presentation Opening (First 60 seconds)

**Purpose:** Hook the audience and frame the entire presentation.

**Template:**
```
[Hook question or statement based on "And"]
[Build tension with "But"]
[Promise resolution with "Therefore" preview]
[Transition to agenda]
```

**Example:**
```
"Every company in this room wants to grow revenue while
improving customer experience. AND the data shows that
companies who do both outperform competitors by 3x.

BUT here's the challenge: most approaches force you to
choose between growth and experience. Fast growth means
cutting corners. Great experience means slow growth.

THEREFORE today I'm going to show you how to achieve both
simultaneously. We'll cover three strategies that our
top-performing clients use to grow 40% faster while
maintaining 95% customer satisfaction.

Let's start with the first strategy..."
```

#### Sales Conversation Opening

**Purpose:** Begin a sales call with narrative engagement.

**Template:**
```
[Discovery AND - Confirm their situation]
[Tension BUT - Introduce or confirm the problem]
[Bridge THEREFORE - Transition to exploration]
```

**Example:**
```
"Based on our earlier conversation, your team is focused on
scaling operations this quarter AND you mentioned efficiency
is a top priority.

BUT you also shared that current tools require too much
manual work, which is slowing things down.

THEREFORE I'd like to explore what's working, what's not,
and see if there's a fit. Can you tell me more about..."
```

**Template:**

**Example:**

Everyone wants to communicate clearly AND we spend hours

### Step 4.2: Add a Supporting Story (Five Primal Elements)

**Instruction:** Create a short anecdote that supports your ABT.

> "Use ABT to set the stage (15 seconds), then Five Elements for the supporting story (45 seconds). Total: Under 90 seconds of compelling narrative."

**The Five Primal Elements:**
1. **Timestamp** - When did this happen?
2. **Location** - Where did it take place?
3. **Character** - Who is this about?
4. **Action** - What happened (including surprise)?
5. **Aha** - What's the insight?

**Template:**
```yaml
five_elements_story:
  timestamp: "[When - be specific]"
  location: "[Where - be concrete]"
  character: "[Who - make relatable]"
  action: "[What happened - include surprise]"
  aha: "[Business insight - connects to ABT]"

  full_story: |
    [Write the 30-45 second story]
```

**Example:**
```
ABT: Companies need consistent content editing BUT building
custom solutions pulls engineering from products THEREFORE
we provide ready components.

STORY:
"Last September [TIMESTAMP] in Austin [LOCATION], I was talking
with a CTO named Maria [CHARACTER]. She told me her team had
spent 8 months building a custom editor - AND it still wasn't
ready [ACTION].

Then she switched to our solution. Six weeks later, she sent
me a message: 'We just launched. My engineers are back on
our core product' [SURPRISE].

That's when I realized: the best solution isn't building
everything yourself - it's knowing what NOT to build [AHA]."
```

### Product Launch
```
[Industry trend] is creating demand for [outcome] AND companies

```

```
[Client] is focused on [priority] AND success requires [capability],
BUT current approaches [problem/limitation],
THEREFORE we propose [service] to achieve [outcome].

```
[Company] is [situation] AND you need [type of person],

```

```

## Troubleshooting Guide

### Problem: "And" is too long
**Symptom:** More than 2-3 clauses in the "And" section
**Fix:** Split into core context + one stake-raiser only

### Problem: "But" feels weak
**Symptom:** Doesn't create tension or urgency
**Fix:** Ask "What keeps them up at night?" and use that

### Problem: "Therefore" is generic
**Symptom:** Could apply to any competitor
**Fix:** Add your unique differentiator or specific outcome

### Problem: Sounds robotic
**Symptom:** Doesn't flow when spoken aloud
**Fix:** Read aloud, adjust for natural speech patterns

### Problem: Audience doesn't engage
**Symptom:** No "lean-in" or follow-up questions
**Fix:** Check if "But" resonates with their actual problems

### Problem: Too long
**Symptom:** Takes more than 30 seconds
**Fix:** Cut each section to one clause, expand later
