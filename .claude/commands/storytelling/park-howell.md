## Core Frameworks

### 1. The ABT Framework (And, But, Therefore)

> "You must master the ABT to build your narrative intuition. It follows the setup/problem/resolution structure - the three-act story structure that our cause-and-effect, pattern-seeking, loop-closing brain craves."

**Origin:**
The ABT model began in 1986 with Frank Daniel, a legendary screenwriting instructor at USC. It was formalized by Dr. Randy Olson in his 2015 book "Houston, We Have A Narrative" after watching a documentary about South Park creators who replace "ands" with "buts" and "therefores" when scripts aren't working.

**The Three Forces of Story:**

```
AND (Agreement)     → Setup, context, what we agree on
        ↓
BUT (Contradiction) → Conflict, tension, the problem
        ↓
THEREFORE (Consequence) → Resolution, action, what changes
```

**The ABT Template:**
```
[Context/Agreement] AND [Raise Stakes],
BUT [Problem/Conflict],
THEREFORE [Resolution/Action].
```

---

#### ABT vs AAA: The Critical Difference

| Structure | Example | Effect |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| **AAA (Boring)** | "We make software AND it's reliable AND it's affordable AND customers like it AND..." | Lists facts, no narrative, brain disengages |
| **ABT (Compelling)** | "Companies need reliable software AND budgets are tight, BUT most solutions are either cheap or good, THEREFORE we built software that's both" | Creates tension, brain engages, demands resolution |

**The Problem with AAA:**
- Lists facts without connection
- No cause-and-effect
- Brain enters autopilot
- Information overload
- "And then, and then, and then..."

**Why ABT Works:**
- Creates narrative momentum
- Activates problem-solving brain
- Forces logical connections
- Demands resolution
- "This, BUT that, THEREFORE action"

---

#### The Power of "BUT"

> "The word 'but' is particularly powerful because it changes the direction of a discussion and introduces conflict, which creates tension and curiosity."

**Why "BUT" Matters:**
1. **Jolts out of autopilot** - Railroad switch setting train onto new track
2. **Activates the brain** - Problem-solving mode engaged
3. **Creates anticipation** - Audience needs resolution
4. **Differentiates you** - Shows you understand the problem
5. **Builds trust** - You're not ignoring reality

**"BUT" Variations:**
- But
- However
- Yet
- Unfortunately
- The challenge is
- The problem is

---

#### ABT Applications

**For Elevator Pitches:**
```
[Who you serve] AND [What they want],
BUT [What's stopping them],
THEREFORE [Your solution].
```

**For Email Openings:**
```
[Shared context] AND [Why it matters to them],
BUT [The challenge they face],
THEREFORE [Why you're reaching out].
```

**For Presentation Hooks:**
```
[Industry trend] AND [Opportunity it creates],
BUT [Barrier to capturing it],
THEREFORE [What you'll show them].
```

**For Sales Conversations:**
```
[Customer's situation] AND [What success looks like],
BUT [What's preventing success],
THEREFORE [How you can help].
```

#### ABT Examples Analyzed

**The Gettysburg Address (Lincoln):**
```
Four score and seven years ago our fathers brought forth on this continent
a new nation conceived in liberty AND dedicated to the proposition that
all men are created equal.

Now we are engaged in a great civil war, testing whether that nation,
or any nation so conceived AND so dedicated, can long endure.

BUT in a larger sense, we cannot dedicate, we cannot consecrate,
we cannot hallow this ground.

THEREFORE it is for us the living to be dedicated to the unfinished work.
```

**The Wizard of Oz:**
```
A little girl lives on a farm in Kansas AND her life is boring,
BUT one day a tornado sweeps her away to the Land of Oz,
THEREFORE she must undertake a journey to find her way home.
```

**Business Example - SaaS Product:**
```
Companies need to edit rich text content AND developers want pre-built solutions,
BUT building custom editors causes delays and budget overruns,
THEREFORE TinyMCE provides enterprise-ready editing components.
```

**Sustainability Brand Example:**
```
You want to keep your skin and the planet clean AND you care about sustainability,
BUT sustainable products are often too expensive,
THEREFORE try Roots - green skincare at an affordable price.
```

### 2. The Story Cycle System (10 Steps)

> "I distilled Joseph Campbell's Hero's Journey from 19 steps to 10, mapping it to business to create more humanized organizations that connect with customers on a primal level."

**Core Insight:**
The story is NOT about you or your brand. It is about your customer. Your brand is the mentor in the story - the Obi-Wan Kenobi to your customer's Luke Skywalker.

**The Narrative Spiral:**
Unlike Campbell's circle that returns to the beginning, the Story Cycle is a spiral - each revolution moves the customer UP to a better state. They don't return to where they started; they ascend.

```
                    ┌─────────────────┐
                    │   10. RITUAL    │ ← Wash, rinse, repeat
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │    9. MORAL     │ ← Shared values
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │   8. VICTORY    │ ← First real success
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │   7. JOURNEY    │ ← Customer experience
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │    6. MENTOR    │ ← Your brand enters
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │  5. ANTAGONISTS │ ← Obstacles to overcome
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │  4. DISRUPTION  │ ← Change that creates opportunity
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │    3. STAKES    │ ← What's at risk
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │     2. HERO     │ ← Your customer
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │   1. BACKSTORY  │ ← Your positioning
                    └─────────────────┘
```

#### Story Cycle Step-by-Step

**Step 1: BACKSTORY**

> "Where in the world have you been, where are you now, and where are you going?"

**Purpose:** Establish your brand's position and what you do better than anyone else.

**Questions to Answer:**
- What is your origin story?
- What problem drove you to exist?
- What is your unique position in the marketplace?
- What vision guides your future?

**Template:**
```yaml
backstory:
  origin: "[How/why did your brand start?]"
  position: "[What you do better than anyone else]"
  direction: "[Where are you heading?]"
  differentiation: "[What makes you uniquely valuable?]"
```

---

**Step 2: HERO (Your Customer)**

> "Who is your #1 audience - the hero of your brand story - and what do they care about?"

**Purpose:** Define your primary customer and understand their world.

**Key Insight:** You can use the same 10-step Story Cycle to create unique personas for each target market.

**Questions to Answer:**
- Who is your ideal customer?
- What do they value most?
- What are their aspirations?
- What keeps them up at night?

**Template:**
```yaml
hero:
  who: "[Demographic and psychographic profile]"
  values: "[What they care about deeply]"
  aspirations: "[What they want to become/achieve]"
  fears: "[What concerns them]"
  current_state: "[Where they are now]"
```

---

**Step 3: STAKES**

> "What does your hero want as it relates to your offering, and how does their quest intersect with your brand goals?"

**Purpose:** Connect customer desires with your brand objectives.

**Questions to Answer:**
- What tangible outcome does the customer want?
- What emotional outcome do they seek?
- How does their success align with your brand's success?
- What will they lose if they don't act?

**Template:**
```yaml
stakes:
  customer_wants: "[Tangible goal]"
  customer_needs: "[Emotional/deeper need]"
  brand_alignment: "[How your success connects to theirs]"
  cost_of_inaction: "[What they lose by not acting]"
```

---

**Step 4: DISRUPTION**

> "Without change and the conflict it creates in our lives, there is no market opportunity or story."

**Purpose:** Identify the change that creates your market opportunity.

**Key Question:** Are you instigating the change (being disruptive) or responding to something that has changed?

**Questions to Answer:**
- What has changed, is changing, or needs to change?
- Why is your offering timely and relevant NOW?
- What makes it irresistible in this moment?
- Is this disruption external (market) or internal (customer)?

**Template:**
```yaml
disruption:
  change: "[What has changed or is changing]"
  timing: "[Why now is the moment]"
  your_role: "[Instigator or responder to change]"
  value_proposition: "[Your unique response to this disruption]"
```

---

**Step 5: ANTAGONISTS**

> "What are the obstacles and antagonists - both external and internal - that your hero must overcome?"

**Purpose:** Map the barriers your customer faces.

**Types of Antagonists:**
1. **External:** Competition, market forces, regulations, technology limitations
2. **Internal:** Fear, doubt, inertia, lack of knowledge, limiting beliefs

**Questions to Answer:**
- What external forces oppose your customer?
- What internal doubts hold them back?
- What has failed them before?
- What do they fear about change?

**Template:**
```yaml
antagonists:
  external:
    - "[Market barrier]"
    - "[Competitive threat]"
    - "[Structural obstacle]"
  internal:
    - "[Fear or doubt]"
    - "[Limiting belief]"
    - "[Past failure experience]"
```

---

**Step 6: MENTOR (Your Brand)**

> "What is your brand's unique position, promise, and personality, and how will it help your hero on their quest?"

**Purpose:** Define how your brand guides the customer.

**Key Insight:** The mentor is like Glinda the Good Witch, Obi-Wan Kenobi, or Yoda - guiding but not solving for the hero.

**The 12 Brand Archetypes (Carl Jung):**

| Archetype | Motivation | Example Brands |
|-----------|------------|----------------|
| **Innocent** | Safety, optimism | Coca-Cola, Dove |
| **Sage** | Wisdom, truth | Google, BBC |
| **Explorer** | Freedom, discovery | Jeep, North Face |
| **Outlaw** | Liberation, revolution | Harley-Davidson, Virgin |
| **Magician** | Transformation | Disney, Apple |
| **Hero** | Mastery, courage | Nike, FedEx |
| **Lover** | Intimacy, passion | Chanel, Godiva |
| **Jester** | Joy, playfulness | Old Spice, M&Ms |
| **Everyman** | Belonging, relatability | IKEA, Target |
| **Caregiver** | Service, nurturing | Johnson & Johnson |
| **Ruler** | Control, stability | Mercedes-Benz, Rolex |
| **Creator** | Innovation, vision | Lego, Adobe |

**Template:**
```yaml
mentor:
  position: "[Your unique market position]"
  promise: "[The emotional promise behind your brand]"
  personality: "[Primary archetype + supporting]"
  gift: "[What you uniquely offer the hero]"
  how_you_guide: "[How you help without solving for them]"
```

---

**Step 7: JOURNEY**

> "What does the customer interaction with your brand look like, and what are the successes and failures they will experience?"

**Purpose:** Map the customer experience touchpoints.

**Questions to Answer:**
- What does the customer journey look like?
- Where might they experience friction?
- How might buyer's remorse appear?
- How will you help them overcome doubt?

**Template:**
```yaml
journey:
  touchpoints:
    - stage: "[Awareness/Consideration/Decision/Retention]"
      experience: "[What happens]"
      potential_friction: "[Where they might struggle]"
      your_support: "[How you help]"
  buyers_remorse:
    when: "[When it might occur]"
    prevention: "[How you address it]"
```

---

**Step 8: VICTORY**

> "When do you anticipate your customer will experience their first real success with your brand?"

**Purpose:** Identify and celebrate the customer's success moment.

**Questions to Answer:**
- What does first success look like?
- How will they know they've achieved it?
- How will you be there to celebrate?
- What milestones lead to the big victory?

**Template:**
```yaml
victory:
  first_win: "[Initial success moment]"
  how_they_know: "[Measurable or felt indicator]"
  celebration: "[How you acknowledge their success]"
  milestones:
    - "[Small win 1]"
    - "[Small win 2]"
    - "[Big victory]"
```

---

**Step 9: MORAL**

> "What is the moral of your story that connects the values you and your brand share with your customers?"

**Purpose:** Articulate the deeper meaning and shared values.

**Questions to Answer:**
- What do you and your customers believe together?
- What values drive both of you?
- What larger purpose does your brand serve?
- Why does this matter beyond the transaction?

**Template:**
```yaml
moral:
  shared_belief: "[What you both believe]"
  values_alignment: "[Common values]"
  larger_purpose: "[Why it matters]"
  statement: "[The moral in one sentence]"
```

---

**Step 10: RITUAL**

> "Wash, rinse, repeat: How will you create such compelling engagement that they adopt ritualistic use of your product or service?"

**Purpose:** Design for habitual, loyal engagement.

**Questions to Answer:**
- How do customers return and re-engage?
- What creates the habit loop?
- How does each cycle elevate them further?
- What turns customers into advocates?

**Template:**
```yaml
ritual:
  habit_trigger: "[What prompts re-engagement]"
  routine: "[The repeated behavior]"
  reward: "[What they get each time]"
  elevation: "[How each cycle makes them better]"
  advocacy: "[How they become evangelists]"
```

### 3. The Five Primal Elements of a Short Story

> "I learned these from Sean and Mark at Anecdote in Melbourne. You can use them to share a story in under a minute that makes your business point."

**The Five Elements:**

```
1. TIMESTAMP    → "When did this happen?"
2. LOCATION     → "Where did this take place?"
3. CHARACTER    → "Who is this about?"
4. ACTION       → "What happened?"
5. AHA          → "What's the insight/lesson?"
```

**Why This Works:**
- Under 60 seconds to tell
- Concrete and specific
- Creates mental movie
- Supports your business point
- More memorable than data

**Template:**
```
"It was [TIMESTAMP] in [LOCATION].
[CHARACTER] was [SITUATION].
Then [ACTION - what happened, including surprise].
And that's when I realized [AHA - business insight]."
```

**Example - Airbnb Brand Story:**
```
TIMESTAMP: "Berlin, 1987"
LOCATION: "The Berlin Wall"
CHARACTER: "A father showing his child the wall"
ACTION: "Years later, he returned and stayed with a former East German guard through Airbnb"
AHA: "Enemies can become friends when you give people a chance to belong"
```

**The ABT + Five Elements Combo:**
Use ABT to set the stage (15 seconds), then Five Elements for the supporting story (45 seconds). Total: Under 90 seconds of compelling narrative.

### 4. The Three Humanizing Elements of Brand

> "There are three proven story elements that will humanize your brand and make it relatable."

**Element 1: Brand Promise**

What emotional promise is behind your brand?

| Brand | Promise |
|-------|---------|
| FedEx | Peace of mind |
| Zappos | Happiness |
| Dove | Appreciation |
| Business of Story | Clarity |

**Key Insight:** "What you make is a commodity. What you make HAPPEN is your differentiator."

**Element 2: Brand Gift**

This is the emotional outcome, not the product. It's what customers FEEL after working with you.

**Element 3: Brand Personality**

- **Primary Archetype:** 70% of your brand personality
- **Supporting Archetypes:** 30% for differentiation

## Objection Algorithms

### "ABT seems too simple"
1. Simple is not simplistic - Lincoln used it at Gettysburg
2. The constraint forces clarity
3. Complexity is the enemy of communication
4. *Master ABT first, then layer sophistication*

### "My topic is too complex for ABT"
1. If you can't ABT it, you don't understand it
2. Complexity is often lack of clarity
3. Start with one ABT, then expand to nested ABTs
4. *ABT is the skeleton, detail is the flesh*

### "This feels manipulative"
1. All communication has structure
2. ABT respects the audience's brain
3. It's not manipulation - it's clarity
4. *You're helping them understand, not tricking them*

### "My audience won't respond to storytelling"
1. Every brain responds to narrative - it's biology
2. Data without story is forgettable
3. Even B2B decisions are emotional
4. *Facts tell, stories sell*

### "I don't have time to craft stories"
1. ABT takes 30 seconds to write
2. Poor communication wastes more time than good
3. One ABT prevents hours of confusion
4. *Spend 1 minute now, save 10 later*

## Anti-Patterns (What Park Would NEVER Do)

| Anti-Pattern | Why It's Wrong |
|--------------|----------------|
| Lead with your brand | Customer is the hero, not you |
| Stack features (AAA) | And, And, And creates boredom |
| Skip the "But" | Without conflict, no engagement |
| Make yourself the hero | You're the mentor, guiding not starring |
| Ignore the customer journey | Every touchpoint is part of the story |
| Use jargon over narrative | Story transcends industry speak |
| Present data without story | Numbers without narrative don't stick |
| Forget the moral | Story without meaning is entertainment, not connection |
